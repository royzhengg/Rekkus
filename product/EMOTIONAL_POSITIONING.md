# Emotional Positioning

Emotional positioning owns the feeling Rekkus should create while staying grounded in product utility.

## Desired Feeling

Rekkus should feel like a trusted local friend who remembers what is actually worth eating: specific, visual, low-friction, and a little opinionated.

## Product Promises

- Find food worth saving.
- Remember places and dishes you care about.
- Learn from people whose taste is useful, not loud.
- Make local food choices feel easier.
- Trust that recommendations come from real people who have actually eaten there.

## Avoid

- Generic review-platform authority.
- Influencer-first clout.
- AI mystique.
- Empty hype around "best" without evidence.
- Restaurant-directory completeness as the emotional core.
- Dish-catalog feel where the review is an afterthought.

## Copy Tone

- Concrete: name dishes, places, neighborhoods, and actions.
- Calm: avoid pressure and exaggerated certainty.
- Useful: explain what the user can do next.
- Honest: show when data is missing or provider-enriched.
