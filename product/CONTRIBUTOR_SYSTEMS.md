# Contributor Systems

Contributor systems own how Rekkus encourages useful food contributions without becoming an influencer platform.

## Contribution Types

| Contribution | Product Value | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Review post | Core content density | Should include place, ratings, and food-specific copy. |
| Dish tags | Dish graph growth | High leverage for discovery. |
| Best dish | Strong ordering signal | Should stay visible on posts/details. |
| Saves | Taste graph input | Private intent unless explicitly shared in future features. |
| Reactions/comments | Lightweight feedback | Lower priority than saves and dish data. |
| Restaurant observations | First-party metadata improvement | Needs trust and moderation path. |

## Contributor Guardrails

- Reward usefulness, specificity, and consistency over follower count.
- Avoid public score mechanics until abuse and moderation foundations exist.
- Keep contribution prompts tied to user intent, visits, saves, or incomplete useful data.
- Do not pressure users to reveal precise location or private routines.
- Treat restaurant owner contributions as distinct from community reviews.

## Future Signals

Potential contributor quality signals:

- Dish specificity rate.
- Review helpfulness or save-follow-on rate.
- Report/moderation history.
- Freshness and local consistency.
- Duplicate/spam contribution rate.

These must remain privacy-safe and explainable before powering ranking or permissions.
